Camp A Mobile App

Camp A is a one stop shop for essential camping equipment.

Project Overview

Description: Camp A is an app with the sole purpose of providing products and information about camping equipment in a quick and easy fashion for users. This app aims to alleviate uncertainty for first-time campers and provides seasoned campers with a simplified process for repeated purchases.

Target Audience: Individuals or families interested in or currently pursuing camping activities

Timeline: 2 Months

Role: Led the entire design process from empathize to testing to the final product

Software: Figma

The Problem

Popular outdoor supply stores offer a wide variety of products and services to choose from. While this may be advantageous for some, users are often overwhelmed by the number and complexity of choices presented to them. Hickâ€™s law states that as the number of options available to a person increases, so does the time it takes for that person to make a decision. Add a three-step checkout process on top of that and users end up spending hours on end shopping online when they could be enjoying the outdoors.

The Solution

Camp A provides users the essentials for a successful and fun-filled camping trip with no extra fluff. A one-step checkout process is easily reachable throughout the app which allows users to get in and out at whatever pace they choose. Previously purchased items are logged and easily accessible for repurchase. If users are new to camping or need a refresher, the tips and tricks page provides helpful information about things like what to bring and what to wear.

User Research

I interviewed 4 people about their experience with supply store apps and their level of interest and participation in camping.

Empathy Map:

User Personas:

Problem Statements:

  • Jim is an avid mountaineer who needs a fast and easy process to order camping items before his trips because he lives in the mountains with spotty service.
  • Joanna is a busy mother who needs to be informed about proper camping equipment that she can buy because she wants to take her family camping for the first time this year.

Hypothesis statements:

  • I believe that a simplified camping supply store app with minimal ordering steps for Jim will allow him to get the tools he needs for his trips in a timely manner.
  • I believe that a camping supply store app that is informational about its products for Joanna will increase her knowledge base so that she feels ready and prepared to go on her trip.

How Might We:

  1. How might we create the quickest checkout process on our camping app?
  2. How might we inform new and expert campers with information that is useful?
  3. How might we give the option to purchase previous items to users of the app?

Reflection

This is one of my most favorite apps I have built so far. I loved having the creative freedom to develop a brand identity from scratch even though it was a lot more work. Everything that I learned from taking the Google UX Design Certificate course was put into this project and I am really proud of it.

Main learning points:

  • Provide users multiple ways to complete an action, as some may be accustomed to gestures that are uncommon to others.
  • Components in figma are extremely useful when you have instances of a design on multiple frames because it helps maintain consistency and is easily alterable.
  • When testing low fidelity prototypes, focus on gaining insight into whether it solves your defined problem and not on feedback that is concerned with how it looks
  • Put bias and personal feelings aside when conducting interviews. This will lead to the most valuable feedback.
  • Do testing even after you have a finished product. Ask yourself, did you succeed?
